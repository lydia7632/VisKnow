VisKnow: Construction and Application of Visual Knowledge Base for Object Understanding

Ziwei Yao, Qiyang Wan, Ruiping Wang, Xilin Chen
Key Laboratory of AI Safety of CAS, Institute of Computing Technology,
Chinese Academy of Sciences(CAS), Beijing, China
Paper Supplementary Code and Data

Multi-modal AnimalKB constructed by the proposed VisKnow framework can be applied in various aspects, including enhancing knowledge-related visual tasks, providing annotations required for constructing benchmarks, and assisting downstream scenarios.

Abstract

Understanding objects is fundamental to computer vision. Although object recognition has achieved remarkable progress, its performance remains suboptimal in data-scarce scenarios such as zero-shot, few-shot, or fine-grained tasks, primarily due to insufficient object understanding. In-depth object understanding encompasses the recognition of internal features (e.g., key appearances and constituent components) and external features (e.g., relationships with other categories and relevant contextual background knowledge), acting as a crucial bridge for enhancing transferability and generalization. It requires visual knowledge annotations beyond basic category labels, closely related to visual elements such as attributes, parts, co-occurrences, and similarities. However, the availability of such knowledge is limited due to the high cost of acquisition. In response, we propose a visual knowledge base construction framework called VisKnow that extracts multi-modal, object-level knowledge for object understanding, through expert design and the utilization of large models to enhance the efficiency. It integrates enriched text-source knowledge, image-source knowledge with region annotations on both object- and part-level, and the alignment between modalities. As a specific case study, we construct AnimalKB, a comprehensive animal knowledge base comprising knowledge extracted from 406 encyclopedic articles, 400k images, and 480k region annotations. Through a series of experiments, we showcase how AnimalKB enhances object-level visual tasks such as zero-shot recognition and fine-grained VQA, serves as outstanding benchmarks for knowledge graph completion and part segmentation tasks. Our findings highlight the potential of automatic construction of visual knowledge bases to further improve visual understanding and the corresponding practical applications.
