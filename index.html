VisKnow: Construction and Application of Multi-Granularity Visual Knowledge Base

Ziwei Yao, Qiyang Wan, Ruiping Wang, Xilin Chen
Key Laboratory of AI Safety of CAS, Institute of Computing Technology,
Chinese Academy of Sciences(CAS), Beijing, China
Paper Supplementary Code and Data

Multi-modal AnimalKB constructed by the proposed VisKnow framework can be applied in various aspects, including enhancing knowledge-related visual tasks, providing annotations required for constructing benchmarks, and assisting downstream scenarios.

Abstract

Knowledge plays a pivotal role in visual understanding tasks, providing a pathway for alignment between AI models and humans. Most existing knowledge bases predominantly contain textual knowledge extracted from corpora and lack visual knowledge entries closely related to visual elements, such as textures and parts. Incorporating such visual knowledge can significantly enhance fine-grained, interpretable, and transferable visual understanding tasks. Visual knowledge remains limited due to its high acquisition cost. In response, we propose a Visual Knowledge base construction framework called VisKnow that extracts multi-modal, multi-granularity knowledge for advanced visual understanding. It integrates enriched text-based knowledge, region-level visual knowledge, and a robust alignment between visual and textual modalities. By leveraging this framework, we construct AnimalKB, a comprehensive animal knowledge base comprising 406 encyclopedic articles, 400k images, and 480k part-level annotations. Through a series of experiments, we showcase how AnimalKB enhances multiple visual tasks, serves as outstanding benchmarks for knowledge graph completion and part segmentation tasks, and helps to build downstream KB-guided applications such as introduction system for museums and nature reserve. Our findings highlight the potential of automatic construction of visual knowledge bases assisted with large models to further improve visual understanding and the corresponding practical applications.
